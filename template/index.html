The Event

LC Xanthi is back with a brand new workshop that everyone awaits!!! So, would you like to learn how to make your life easier? Are you interested in using Internet οf Τhings in order to create a Smart House? Well, we have the answer for you! In our third international workshop that will take place from the 15th to 22th of May, you’ll have the chance to create your own “House of Apps”, a Smart House that will help you save energy and time, something that we lack nowadays. Grab the opportunity to explore and conquer your “White House” without letting devices “conspire” against you. But what is a Smart House?With the help of the Internet of Things, simple household devices can be accessed digitally. This way they are able to generate, exchange and consume data with minimal human intervention. In this event you will have the opportunity to use the Internet of Things in order to create a Smart House, a house that incorporates advanced automation systems to provide the inhabitants with sophisticated monitoring and control over the building's functions. Moreover, you will create different applications step-by-step, that will give you the power to control your electronic devices. At the end of our workshop, you will have the opportunity to install your inventions in a graphical house of the Old town of Xanthi! Τhere is no need to be an expert in order to join our event, since we are going to start from scratch!So, is that all?Of course not! There are so much more you can enjoy in Xanthi! Xanthi is one of the most beautiful cities in northern Greece. You will have the chance to wander through the streets of the picturesque Old Town, which offers many exciting options either for a morning coffee or a memorable nightout! Meet new people, make new friends and experience the famous Greek hospitality. Explore the “Path of Life”, a beautiful getaway from the urban city and walk in the forest to see the waterfalls and enjoy magnificent views.It’s not random that Xanthi is called “The city of 1000 colors”. Furthermore, the sunny weather of May will give you the chance to swim in the beautiful sea of Greece, and spend some time under the sun. Last but not least, while you are in Xanthi, you can taste a lot of local products: wake up with a tasty greek coffee and eat delicious local food while drinking traditional Ouzo, Mastiha & Tsipouro! Still in doubt? Xanthi is the place to be this May! Prepare your elections campaign and fight your way to the Oval Room of Xanthi’s “House of Apps”

Old City

The old town of Xanthi built in the northern part of the modern city right on the southern slope of the mountain. The old city is a whole world and the reality can not be described or captured in any way. So we suggest you to see closely this beauty that has remained from an another era.

Culture

Χanthi, the city of dreams and creativity, combines tradition with modern expression, highlighting the rich customs and traditions, but also it’s long history. Concerning the museums ,in Xanthi you are able to visit a variety of them ,such as the Museum of Natural History, the Municipal Art Gallery, the Archeological Museum and many others.

Entertainment

Xanthi is undoubtedly famous for it ‘s amusement, as well as the plethora of options offered for coffee or a drink. The unique and cozy fun, is something that becomes unforgettable for everyone.

Food

Xanthi has a big tradition in food. The spicy meals, affected by the Turkish cousine offers you a different taste that will be very difficult for you to forget. Also, Xanthi’s traditional sweets are very tastefull and can satisfy the most demanding customers !

Bazaar

The history of the bazaar of Xanthi is long, lost in the years of the Ottoman period. Unfolding it, there are many detailed stories of earlier travelers in Thrace, with the first of these found in the 15th century.

Republic Square

The Central Square(or Republic Square) itself is an attraction, since in recent years began to regaining its former glory. This square was first designed in 1870 and since then began to receive many contributions, culminating in the late 1880s when the big clock was built.
